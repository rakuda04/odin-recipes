Pizza Slider

I've always loved pizza, but it wasn't until I was in college that I really learned how to make it properly. My roommates and I were always looking for ways to entertain our friends, and we found that pizza sliders were a great way to do it. They were easy to make, portable, and everyone loved them.

We started by experimenting with different recipes, and we eventually settled on one that we all loved. The key to our pizza sliders is using a combination of cheeses, including mozzarella, provolone, and Parmesan. We also add a touch of Italian seasoning for some extra flavor.

The first time I made pizza sliders from scratch, I was a little nervous. I didn't want to mess them up, but I was determined to give it a try. I followed the recipe carefully, and I was so happy with the results. The pizza sliders were cheesy, flavorful, and everyone loved them.

Since then, I've made pizza sliders many times, and I've always gotten rave reviews. They've become a staple in my repertoire, and I love sharing them with friends and family.

    • 1 ½ cups finely pre-shredded low-moisture part-skim mozzarella cheese
    • 1 cup finely pre-shredded Italian cheese blend
    • ¼ cup finely chopped fresh basil
    • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
    • 2 teaspoons dried Italian seasoning
    • 1 ⅓ cups pizza sauce
    • 2 tablespoons sun-dried tomato pesto
    • 1 (12 count) package Hawaiian sweet rolls
    • ¾ cup (about 3 oz.) thinly sliced pepperoni
    • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
    • 1 tablespoon grated Parmesan cheese
    • ¾ teaspoon garlic powder
